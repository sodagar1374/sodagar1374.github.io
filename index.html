About

I am a PhD Candidate in Statistics at Rutgers University, specializing in statistical machine learning, high-dimensional inference, and deep generative models. With 3+ years of quantitative finance experience at Rabobank (New York), I have built sophisticated forecasting and risk management tools for derivatives portfolios.

My current research interests span dynamic factor models and sparse state-space inference, VAEs, normalizing flows, and diffusion models, causal inference in finance and healthcare, and machine-learning–based stochastic weather generators for renewable energy applications.

Research Areas

🔄 Dynamic Factor Models

Identifiable dynamic factor models with episodic factor activation and efficient MAP-EM/Kalman filtering techniques for time series analysis.

🧠 Deep Generative Models

VAEs, normalizing flows, and diffusion models for modeling complex temporal structures and stochastic volatility in financial data.

⚡ Causal Machine Learning

Combining causal inference with deep learning for analyzing order book dynamics and clinical EEG pattern recognition.

🌤️ Weather Generation

Minute-level wind vector generation models trained on multi-decade atmospheric datasets for renewable energy forecasting.
